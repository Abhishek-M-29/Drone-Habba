Event 1: Drone Competition
STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)
1. Purpose & Scope
To ensure standardized, safe, and transparent conduct of the Student Drone Competition, including civil and defense-oriented UAV mission categories. This SOP governs registration, technical validation, operational control, evaluation, and compliance procedures.
2. Eligibility & Registration
•	Open to Diploma/UG/PG students from recognized institutions.
•	Team size: 3–6 members.
•	Valid institutional ID mandatory.
•	Submission of technical specification sheet and mission profile (mandatory for Defense category).
3. Technical Compliance
•	Maximum Takeoff Weight (MTOW)
•	Mandatory pre-flight inspection and weight verification.
•	Approved RF frequency allocation only.
•	Functional failsafe/kill switch required.
•	Defense category UAVs must include mission-relevant systems (e.g., surveillance camera, GPS logging, encrypted telemetry if applicable).
4. Safety & Operational Control
•	Defined flying zone with restricted spectator access and safety perimeter.
•	Mandatory safety and mission briefing before flight.
•	On-site fire extinguisher, first-aid, and emergency response team.
•	Any unsafe maneuver or violation results in immediate disqualification.
5. Competition Categories & Evaluation
A. Civil Categories:
•	Racing / Obstacle Navigation
•	Payload Delivery
•	Autonomous Waypoint Mission
B. Defense Category (Simulation-Based):
•	Surveillance & Reconnaissance Mission
•	Target Identification / Geo-tagging
•	Precision Supply Drop (simulated logistics support)
Evaluation based on:
•	Mission accuracy
•	Completion time
•	Stability & control
•	Technical innovation
•	Safety compliance
6. Results, Awards & Governance
•	Scores verified and approved by Chief Judge.
•	Decision of Organizing Committee is final and binding.
•	Awards: Overall Winner, Category Winners, Best Defense UAV, Best Innovation, Best Safety Compliance.
•	Post-event documentation and technical records maintained for institutional reference
Budget required 
1. Venue & Setup
•	Ground preparation, barricading, safety netting
•	Stage, sound system, banners
2. Technical Arrangements
•	Race gates / obstacle setup
•	Timing system & scoring setup
3. Safety & Operations
•	Fire extinguishers & first aid
•	Safety marshals & security
•	Power supply & charging stations
4. Judges & Officials
•	Honorarium for 2–3 experts

5. Awards & Certificates
6. Miscellaneous & Contingency
•	Photography & documentation


Event 2: Investigation on Aircraft Accident
STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)
1. Competition Activation & Case Release
•	Official accident scenario (with FDR, CVR transcript, weather data, maintenance logs) shall be released to teams at the start of the competition.
•	Teams must classify the occurrence as per ICAO Annex 13 (Accident / Serious Incident).
•	Investigation timeline and submission deadline shall be strictly enforced.
2. Evidence Handling & Site Documentation
•	Teams must prepare a structured wreckage documentation plan (sketch, impact sequence, debris distribution).
•	Chain-of-custody protocol must be demonstrated for critical evidence (CVR/FDR).
•	All assumptions must be clearly differentiated from verified evidence.
3. Technical & Operational Analysis
•	Systematic analysis of:
o	Flight Data Recorder parameters
o	Cockpit Voice Recorder transcript
o	ATC communication & weather data
•	Evaluation of aircraft systems, maintenance history, and operational compliance.
4. Human Factors & Root Cause Determination
•	Assessment of pilot qualifications, CRM, fatigue, and decision-making.
•	Identification of technical, environmental, and organizational contributing factors.
•	Determination of Probable Cause supported strictly by evidence.
5. Reporting, Presentation & Award Criteria
•	Team Size 3-5 members.
•	Submission of structured investigation report (3-5 pages recommended).
•	Presentation before expert panel (10–15 minutes).
•	Evaluation Criteria:
o	Technical accuracy (30%)
o	Logical reasoning & evidence linkage (25%)
o	Depth of analysis (20%)
o	Practical safety recommendations (15%)
o	Professional documentation & presentation (10%)
Best Investigation Procedure Award shall be given to the team demonstrating the most systematic, evidence-driven, and ICAO-compliant investigation methodology.
Budget Requirement
1. Venue & Technical Setup
•	Hall, projector, printing of case materials

2. Judges & Awards
•	Expert honorarium + trophies + certificates

3. Documentation & Miscellaneous
•	Printing, photography, contingency

















Event 3: Poster Presentation
STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)
Theme: Latest Innovations in Aeronautical / Aerospace Engineering
1. Theme Definition, Eligibility & Registration
•	Theme: Latest Innovations in Aeronautical / Aerospace Engineering (e.g., Sustainable Aviation Fuel, Electric/Hybrid Propulsion, Hypersonic Vehicles, UAV Swarm Technology, Space Propulsion, AI in Aviation, Advanced Composites, Reusable Launch Systems).
•	Open to Diploma/UG/PG students (Individual or Team of maximum 3 members).
•	Abstract submission (250–300 words) with innovation focus and application relevance is mandatory.
•	Abstract screening will be conducted by Technical Committee prior to final selection.
2. Poster Technical Specifications & Content Requirements
•	Poster Size: A1 (594 mm × 841 mm), Portrait orientation.
•	Mandatory Sections:
o	Title & Authors (with Institution)
o	Problem Statement / Industry Gap
o	Description of Innovation
o	Technical Methodology / Concept Model
o	Performance Benefits (with data/figures)
o	Future Scope & Applications
•	Minimum font size: 24 pt (readable at 1–1.5 m distance).
•	Graphs, performance charts, CAD images, system block diagrams encouraged.
•	All data must be referenced; plagiarism is strictly prohibited.
3. Presentation & Evaluation Procedure
•	Presentation Time: 7–8 minutes + 3 minutes Q&A.
•	Posters shall be displayed in designated panels.
•	Judges will independently evaluate using structured score sheets.
Evaluation Criteria (100 Marks)
•	Technical Innovation & Originality – 30
•	Engineering Feasibility & Practical Application – 25
•	Analytical Depth & Data Support – 20
•	Clarity of Presentation & Communication – 15
•	Visual Quality & Professional Formatting – 10

4. Compliance, Ethics & Professional Conduct
•	Professional attire mandatory during presentation.
•	Proper citation required for all referenced research.
•	Confidential or classified aerospace data must not be used.
•	Late submissions or non-compliance with format guidelines may lead to disqualification.
•	Decision of Jury Committee shall be final.
5. Awards & Recognition
•	Best Innovation Poster Award
•	Runner-Up Award
•	Special Recognition (Sustainability / Space Technology / Defense Innovation)
•	Certificates for all participants
Best Poster shall be awarded to the team presenting the most technically sound, industry-relevant, and future-oriented aerospace innovation.
Budget Requirement

•	Poster stands/panels, hall setup, printing assistance

2. Judges & Awards

Event 4: Egg Parachute
STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)

1. Objective & Eligibility
•	Objective: Design and fabricate a parachute system capable of safely landing a raw egg dropped from a specified height (e.g., 10–20 meters) without damage.
•	Open to Diploma/UG/PG students.
•	Team size: 2–4 members.
•	Each team may submit only one entry.
2. Design & Technical Specifications
•	Maximum system weight: e.g., ≤500 grams (including egg).
•	Parachute must be self-deploying (no manual assistance after release).
•	Allowed materials: fabric, plastic sheets, paper, strings, and biodegradable materials.
•	Prohibited: metal cages, pre-packaged protective shells, shock-absorbing gels.
•	Egg must be visible and unaltered before test.
3. Competition Procedure
•	Pre-drop inspection for compliance and weight check.
•	Drop height: 10–20 meters (building/controlled platform).
•	Each team allowed one official drop (one trial if specified).
•	Egg integrity checked immediately after landing.
•	Time of descent may be recorded (optional bonus scoring).
4. Evaluation Criteria (100 Marks)
•	Egg Survival (No Crack) – 40
•	Descent Stability & Controlled Landing – 20
•	Design Innovation & Engineering Concept – 20
•	Weight Efficiency – 10
•	Team Explanation & Technical Justification – 10
Disqualification for rule violation or unsafe design.
5. Safety, Awards & Compliance
•	Drop zone secured with safety perimeter.
•	Only authorized personnel allowed near landing area.
•	Decision of judges is final and binding.
•	Awards: Winner, Runner-Up, Best Innovative Design.
Best Design Award may be given to the most efficient and aerodynamically optimized parachute system.
Budget Requirement 
1. Infrastructure & Setup
•	Drop platform access, barricading, weighing scale

2. Judges & Awards
•	Honorarium + trophies + certificates

3. Logistics & Miscellaneous
•	Eggs, documentation, contingency

















Event 5: Design of Aircraft Parameters (Wing and Fuselage)
STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)

1. Objective & Eligibility
•	Objective: To design and justify optimal wing and fuselage parameters for a specified mission profile (e.g., 4-seater trainer aircraft / UAV / regional transport).
•	Open to Diploma / UG / PG students (Team of 2–4 members).
•	Each team shall design based on the provided design requirements (range, payload, cruise speed, altitude, etc.).
•	One submission per team.
2. Design Requirements & Technical Scope
Teams must calculate and justify:
Wing Parameters
•	Wing area (S)
•	Aspect ratio (AR)
•	Wingspan (b)
•	Taper ratio & sweep (if applicable)
•	Airfoil selection with justification
•	Wing loading (W/S)
Fuselage Parameters
•	Fuselage length & diameter
•	Fineness ratio
•	Cabin layout / payload arrangement
•	Structural configuration concept
Design must be supported by analytical calculations, assumptions, and references.
3. Submission & Presentation Format
•	Technical report (10–15 pages recommended) including:
o	Mission specification
o	Design methodology
o	Sizing calculations
o	Performance estimation
o	CAD model or layout sketch
•	Presentation: 10 minutes + 5 minutes Q&A.
•	Soft copy and printed summary required.
4. Evaluation Criteria (100 Marks)
•	Accuracy of Engineering Calculations – 30
•	Design Methodology & Assumptions – 20
•	Feasibility & Performance Justification – 20
•	Innovation & Optimization Approach – 15
•	Technical Presentation & Documentation – 15
Penalty for unrealistic assumptions or unsupported data.
5. Compliance, Awards & Governance
•	Standard atmosphere and aerodynamic relations must be clearly stated.
•	All data sources must be referenced.
•	Judges’ decision shall be final.
•	Awards:
o	Best Overall Aircraft Design
o	Best Aerodynamic Optimization
o	Best Technical Presentation
Best Design Award will be given to the team demonstrating optimal balance between aerodynamic efficiency, structural feasibility, and mission performance.
